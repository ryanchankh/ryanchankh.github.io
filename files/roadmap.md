# My Research Roadmap
*I have been meaning to keep a list of research topic "phases" since the beginning of my research journey. Hopefully this gives a good idea to people interested in pursuing a research career what it's like. I'll try to update this as often as I can.*

- [06/2020] Worked on a paper with Yi and Yaodong Yu. Just submitted to NeurIPS. More info on that later.

- [05/2020] Started my job at LLNL. People are friendly and got 3 very hands on project. Started looking into Gaussian Processes and time series data. 

- [01/2020] Even though I graduated, I often go to school and study at Yi's lab. Got a lot of opportunity to talk to Yi and Chong You. There are a lot of smart people at Yi's lab. 

- [11/2019] I stayed an extra semester at Berkeley to learn more fun math like differential topology. I submitted my paper to NICE workshop. Took a really fun class with Yi Ma (High Dim Data Analysis using Low Dim Models) that semester, so I started working on Dictionary Learning with Simon at Yi's Lab.  

- [05/2019] Focused on learning sparse coding theory, took two neuroscience classes. Got a pretty basic idea of how vision works in humans. Started working on a paper with Dylan; paper is called Subspace Locally Compeitive Algorithm. Also trying to catch up with Information Theory and Bayes Inference (just a bit). 

- [12/2019] Finished my first Intro to Machine Learning Class.

- [08/2018] Summer internship at 51Job. Mainly focused on neural network parser.

- [06/2018] Just started research in neuroscience and machine learning. My first exposure was working on replicating style transfer. Getting my ass kicked at Berkeley, and disappointing a lot of professors since I didn't know anything really.

- [06/2017] Just started research in neuroscience and machine learning. Started attending Redwood meetings and met Dylan. My first exposure was working on replicating style transfer. Picking up python and tensorflow. 

- [ancient] Didn't care too much about research. I was studying pure math at the time. But I collaborated a math research seminar at PCC with Benjamin Kim, who is working on a startup now.
